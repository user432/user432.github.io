Research

The goal of my research is to deploy robots, or embodied AI agents more broadly, in various non-factory real-world settings to perform diverse tasks. To that end, I am interested in:

  • Passive Internet Data: How do we make use of broad sources of passive data that exist on the web, like videos of humans and natural language to enable more effective learning in our robots?
  • Real-world Robotic Data: How do we design self-supervised RL policies that can consume such broad data (Open X-Embodiment), which may come from non-experts and lack reward labels, and from it learn to reach unseen goals.
  • Simulated Robotic Data: Instead of relying solely on real-world experience, can we gather experience by simulating robot interactions in virtual environments?


I have recently grown an interest in humanoid robots. Why Humanoid robots?
This materialistic world we live in, and the multitude of tools we've built till now are mostly designed for the human-like form with 2 arms, 2 legs, and hands. While there have been a lot of advancements in parallel or suction grippers, they will stall when faced with “simple” challenges such as securely picking up and holding a coffee mug. This makes humanoids well-suited for tasks that require mobility, dexterity, adaptability, and the ability to operate in complex and dynamic environments. To add to that, we have already accumulated hours of video data (such as Ego4D, EPIC-Kitchens) of humans interacting with and performing many tasks. So,
  • How can robots acquire the ability to navigate and interact with dynamic and unpredictable environments?
  • How can robots acquire the ability to learn from human demonstrations and generalize that knowledge to new scenarios?
  • How can robots learn to manipulate objects with varying material properties, such as soft and deformable objects?
